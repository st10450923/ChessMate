Chess Mate!

A two-phase strategy game for two players

Genre 2-Player Strategy
Platform PC — Unity Engine
Jam Theme Switch
Author Ember Jones
Document v1.0 — Day 2
♟ Chess Phase ⇄ Switch ● Checkers Phase
Elevator Pitch

One board, two games. The rules switch between chess and checkers every two turns — pieces keep their location and identity but move and attack differently each phase. Adapt your strategy around the changing board state to outsmart your opponent and master both games at once.

Core Concept

The game alternates between two phases. Once each player has taken one turn in the current phase, the board switches. Chess pieces retain their positions and identities across every switch, but their movement and capture rules transform entirely with the phase.
Phase One
Chess
All standard chess movement applies — each piece moves according to its identity: Pawn, Rook, Knight, Bishop, Queen, or King. En passant and castling are supported.
Win by: Capturing the opponent's King
Phase Two
Checkers
All pieces move diagonally as checkers discs. Mandatory captures apply. Multi-jump chains are allowed. A piece reaching the back rank becomes a Checkers King for that phase.
Win by: Capturing all opponent pieces, capturing the King, or leaving opponent with no valid moves

Gameplay Loop

W
Chess Phase — Turn 1
Player 1 (White) moves a piece according to chess rules
B
Chess Phase — Turn 2
Player 2 (Black) moves a piece according to chess rules
⇄
Switch
The board flips — all pieces become checkers discs. Positions are preserved.
W
Checkers Phase — Turn 1
Player 1 moves a piece according to checkers rules. Captures are mandatory.
B
Checkers Phase — Turn 2
Player 2 moves. Multi-jump chains may extend the turn.
⇄
Switch
Board flips back. Pieces revert to their chess identities. The cycle repeats until a win condition is met.

Visual Style

Chess Phase
Detailed piece sprites showing each type clearly — Rook, Knight, Bishop, and so on. Classic board palette. Icons sourced from game-icons.net.
Checkers Phase
Minimalist disc sprites with a small chess icon inset to preserve identity. Bolder, flatter colour palette signals the rule change visually.
The Switch Animation
Board squeezes flat on the X axis, sprites swap at the midpoint, board expands. Pieces briefly hide during the flip and reappear in new form.
Asset Sources
game-icons.net and kenney.nl — free, open-licence assets. No AI-generated art used, ensuring a consistent visual style throughout.
